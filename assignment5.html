Moving Parts!

For this assignment I wanted to create a box with a lid mechanism that slides into the box and flaps down. In OnShape I started with an extruded square and used thicken to make the solid. Then I created another added extrusion for the box butt. It took a little while to work out how I was going to construct it but after consulting Joshua I worked out that I would 3D print all the parts.

While 3D printing I had a problem copying my file to the printer and I think the problem was that if you export all of your parts at once then it causes that error. I exported all the parts one by one and was able to copy files to the printer.

Joshua gave me advice that I should add a bottom section when printing the small connectors. That setting is called raft in the building plate adhesion section. These parts printed smoothly and snapping off the part was really satisfying.

There was no gluestick when I started printing my cover so it didn't stick to the bottom and one of the corners lifted during printing. Also, the cover printed as hollow and with no top. I'm a little confused, but I will ask Joshua in class what happened.
